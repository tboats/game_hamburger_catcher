# Template: Blog Post

> For blog articles — informative, engaging, SEO-friendly.
> Target length: 80–300 lines.

## Standard Structure

```markdown
# [Blog Title — Clear, Keyword-Rich]

> **Published:** YYYY-MM-DD | **Author:** [Name]
> **Tags:** [tag1], [tag2], [tag3]
> **Reading time:** ~[N] min

---

## TL;DR

[2-3 sentences capturing the core message. Readers who stop here still get value.]

---

## [Opening Hook]

[Start with a relatable problem, question, or surprising fact. Draw the reader in within 2-3 sentences.]

## [Section 1: Context / Background]

[Set the stage. Why does this matter?]

## [Section 2: Core Insight / Solution]

[Main content. Use subheadings, bullet points, and code blocks for scannability.]

### [Subsection if needed]

[...]

## [Section 3: Practical Takeaway]

[Actionable advice. What should the reader do next?]

## Conclusion

[Wrap up in 2-3 sentences. End with a call-to-action or thought-provoking question.]

---

_[Optional: Related posts, further reading links]_
```

## Additional Rules

1. **TL;DR required:** Every blog post MUST start with a TL;DR section after the title.
2. **Scannable:** Use short paragraphs (max 4 lines), bullet points, and subheadings liberally.
3. **Hook first:** Opening paragraph must grab attention — no generic introductions.
4. **One idea per post:** Stay focused on a single core message. Split into series if needed.
5. **CTA:** End with a clear call-to-action (comment, share, try it, read next).
